About Me

Who I Am

I'm a final-semester Civil & Environmental Engineering student at Shahjalal University of Science and Technology (SUST), Sylhet, Bangladesh. My academic journey has been built on a solid foundation of structural analysis, environmental impact assessment, and GIS-based spatial computing.

My undergraduate thesis focuses on a GIS-based Life Cycle Assessment of plastic waste-to-energy conversion through pyrolysis — a project that blends environmental science, spatial analysis, and Python automation to develop a sustainable urban waste management framework for Sylhet City.

Beyond my core engineering skills, I'm passionate about technology. I've completed formal web development training and bring a unique blend of civil engineering expertise and programming capability to every project I work on.

Research & Projects

🔬 Ongoing — Nov 2025 to Present
GIS-Based Life Cycle Assessment of Plastic Waste-to-Energy Conversion through Pyrolysis: A Sustainable Framework for Urban Waste Management in Sylhet
Student Researcher · Shahjalal University of Science and Technology
  • Utilized ArcGIS Pro to analyze ward-wise plastic waste generation in Sylhet City based on population density and waste generation data.
  • Conducted site selection and suitability analysis for waste-to-energy conversion plants using spatial multi-criteria methods.
  • Integrated Python scripts within ArcGIS Pro to streamline and automate data processing and spatial analysis workflows.
  • Performing a full Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) via OpenLCA to compare three different pyrolysis routes for environmental impact.
  • Identifying the most environmentally sustainable pyrolysis method by evaluating emissions, energy, and material flow.
  • Developing a comprehensive, scalable framework for urban waste management that reduces environmental footprint across the city.
